Super built-up

Super built-up refers to the total area of an apartment, including its carpet area and a proportionate share of common areas.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, the term 'Super built-up' is commonly used to describe the total area of a residential unit. This includes not just the carpet area, which is the actual usable area within the walls of the apartment, but also a share of the common areas of the building. Common areas can include corridors, staircases, elevators, and recreational spaces. The super built-up area is calculated by adding the carpet area and the proportionate share of these common areas. This measurement is important for buyers as it gives a more comprehensive view of the total space they are purchasing. However, it can sometimes lead to confusion, as the super built-up area is often larger than the carpet area, which can mislead potential buyers regarding the actual usable space. Understanding super built-up area helps buyers make informed decisions and evaluate the value of a property more accurately.

Examples

  • An apartment with a carpet area of 1000 sq. ft. and a super built-up area of 1200 sq. ft. indicates 200 sq. ft. of common area.
  • In a project with amenities like a gym and swimming pool, the super built-up area includes a share of these spaces.

Additional Information

  • Developers often advertise properties using super built-up area for marketing purposes.
  • Buyers should clarify how the super built-up area is calculated to avoid discrepancies.
