Capital gain

The profit realized from the sale of a property or asset.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a capital gain refers to the increase in the value of a property when it is sold compared to its purchase price. This profit is subject to taxation under the Income Tax Act in India. Capital gains can be classified into two categories: short-term and long-term. If a property is sold within two years of acquisition, the gains are considered short-term and taxed at the individual's income tax slab rate. If the property is held for more than two years, it qualifies as a long-term capital gain, which is taxed at a reduced rate of 20% after indexation benefits. Indexation allows the adjustment of the purchase price for inflation, thus reducing the taxable income. Understanding capital gains is crucial for investors as it directly impacts the net profit from real estate investments and also influences decisions regarding buying, holding, or selling properties.

Examples

  • An individual purchases a flat for ₹50 lakhs and sells it for ₹75 lakhs after 5 years, realizing a long-term capital gain of ₹25 lakhs.
  • A property bought for ₹30 lakhs is sold for ₹32 lakhs within 1 year, resulting in a short-term capital gain of ₹2 lakhs.

Additional Information

  • Investors can minimize capital gains tax through exemptions under Section 54 and 54F for reinvesting in residential properties.
  • The holding period for determining short-term and long-term capital gains was reduced from 3 years to 2 years for real estate in the 2017 budget.
