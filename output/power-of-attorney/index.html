Power of attorney

A legal document that allows one person to act on behalf of another in real estate transactions.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a Power of Attorney (POA) is a legal instrument that grants an individual (the attorney-in-fact) the authority to act on behalf of another person (the principal) regarding property transactions. This can include buying, selling, leasing, or managing real estate assets. The POA is particularly useful in situations where the principal is unable to be physically present to handle transactions due to various reasons such as residing abroad or being incapacitated. There are two types of POA: a general power of attorney, which grants broad powers, and a specific power of attorney, which is limited to certain actions. It is important that the POA is executed properly, adhering to the legal requirements in India, including registration in some cases, to ensure it is enforceable. Additionally, the POA must be unconditional and clear in its terms to avoid disputes and legal complications.

Examples

  • A property owner appoints a relative as their attorney-in-fact to sell their property while they are living abroad.
  • An investor uses a power of attorney to allow a real estate agent to manage multiple property transactions on their behalf.

Additional Information

  • A Power of Attorney can be revoked by the principal at any time, provided they are mentally competent.
  • It's advisable to consult a legal expert when drafting a Power of Attorney to ensure compliance with local laws.
