Earnest money

A deposit made to demonstrate a buyer's serious intent to purchase a property.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, earnest money refers to a sum of money that a buyer offers to a seller as a show of good faith when entering into a transaction. This amount is typically a small percentage of the total purchase price and is submitted along with the offer to purchase. The primary purpose of earnest money is to signify the buyer's commitment to completing the transaction, thus providing the seller with some assurance that the buyer is serious. If the deal goes through, the earnest money is usually applied to the purchase price. However, if the transaction falls through due to the buyer's fault, the seller may retain the earnest money as compensation for the time and effort expended. Conversely, if the seller fails to meet the terms of the agreement, the earnest money is often returned to the buyer. The amount of earnest money can vary depending on the market conditions and the negotiations between the buyer and seller.

Examples

  • A buyer offers ₹2 lakh as earnest money for a property worth ₹50 lakh.
  • If the buyer backs out after making a ₹1 lakh earnest money deposit, the seller may keep the deposit.

Additional Information

  • Earnest money is typically held in an escrow account until the transaction is completed.
  • The amount of earnest money can be negotiated between the buyer and seller.
