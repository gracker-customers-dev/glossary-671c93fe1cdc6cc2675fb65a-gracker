Ground rent

Ground rent is a periodic payment made by a leaseholder to the freeholder for the use of land.

Description

In the Indian real estate context, ground rent refers to the rent paid by a lessee (tenant) to the lessor (landowner) for the use of land on which the tenant may build or utilize a structure. This arrangement is common in leasehold properties, where the land is owned by one party while the building is owned by another. Ground rent agreements typically last for a long term, often 99 years or more, and the rent is usually a nominal amount, fixed or subject to periodic reviews. The concept can be beneficial for both parties: the landowner receives a steady income while retaining ownership of the land, and the tenant has the opportunity to develop or use the property without the high upfront costs of purchasing land. In India, ground rent agreements must comply with local laws and regulations, and they play a significant role in urban development, particularly in planned estates and developments.

Examples

  • A commercial developer leases land from a government authority and pays an annual ground rent for a shopping complex built on that land.
  • A residential leaseholder pays ground rent to the landowner for a plot on which they have constructed their home.

Additional Information

  • Ground rent can be subject to escalation clauses, allowing for periodic increases based on market rates.
  • In some cases, ground rent can lead to disputes over payments and lease terms, making it essential to have clear agreements.
