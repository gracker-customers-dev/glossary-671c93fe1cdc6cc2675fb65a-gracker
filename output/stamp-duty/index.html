Stamp duty

A tax imposed on legal documents, usually in the context of property transactions.

Description

Stamp duty is a tax levied by the government on the transfer of property ownership or the execution of certain documents related to real estate transactions in India. This tax is calculated as a percentage of the property's market value or the transaction value, whichever is higher. The rates of stamp duty vary from state to state and are determined by local governments. In addition to the purchase price of the property, stamp duty is applicable on other documents such as agreements for sale, conveyance deeds, and lease agreements. Payment of stamp duty is mandatory for the registration of property documents, and failure to pay it can lead to penalties or legal complications. The proceeds from stamp duty contribute to state revenue and are used for various public expenditures. Buyers should be well aware of the applicable stamp duty rates and ensure that it is considered as part of their overall transaction costs when purchasing property.

Examples

  • If a property is purchased for ₹50 lakhs and the stamp duty rate is 7%, the stamp duty payable would be ₹3.5 lakhs.
  • For a lease agreement with a monthly rent of ₹30,000, if the stamp duty is 2% of the total rent for the lease term, the stamp duty would be calculated on the total of the lease duration.

Additional Information

  • Certain exemptions or concessions on stamp duty may apply for first-time homebuyers.
  • Stamp duty is often a significant cost in real estate transactions and should be factored into the buyer's budget.
