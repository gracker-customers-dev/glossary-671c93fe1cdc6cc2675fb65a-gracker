Marla

A unit of area commonly used in real estate in India and Pakistan.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a Marla is a traditional unit of area that is widely used to measure plots of land. The size of a Marla can vary regionally, but it is generally accepted to be equivalent to approximately 272.25 square feet or 25.3 square meters. In some areas, particularly in Punjab, a Marla is often defined as 225 square feet. The term is commonly used in property listings, sales agreements, and real estate transactions, especially in rural and semi-urban settings. Marla has historical significance and is part of a system of land measurement that includes other units such as Kanal and Biswa. Understanding the Marla is crucial for buyers and sellers in the Indian real estate market as it provides a standard for measuring land, which can affect pricing, property development, and legal documentation. As the real estate landscape evolves, the understanding and usage of Marla remain essential for stakeholders in property transactions.

Examples

  • A 5 Marla plot is often considered a standard size for residential construction.
  • Properties in urban areas might be listed as 10 Marla, indicating a larger plot suitable for more extensive homes.

Additional Information

  • Marla measurements can differ slightly between regions, so it's important to verify local standards.
  • In some contexts, Marla is also used in agricultural land measurement.
