Cooperative

A type of housing ownership where residents collectively own and manage the property.

Description

In the Indian real estate context, a cooperative, often referred to as a housing cooperative or housing society, is a legal entity formed by a group of individuals who come together to own and manage residential properties. Each member of the cooperative holds shares in the society, which entitles them to occupy a specific unit or apartment within the property. The cooperative is governed by a set of bylaws, and decisions regarding maintenance, finances, and operations are made collectively by the members through elections and meetings. This model fosters a sense of community and shared responsibility, as members are not just tenants but also stakeholders in the property. Cooperative housing societies are popular in urban areas, offering an affordable alternative to individual ownership. The cooperative structure can help in managing resources efficiently and provides a framework for resolving disputes among members. Members typically pay a monthly maintenance fee that covers building upkeep, utilities, and other common expenses.

Examples

  • A group of families forms a cooperative to build and manage a residential complex in Mumbai.
  • An old cooperative housing society in Delhi redevelops its property to modernize facilities.

Additional Information

  • Cooperatives often require members to adhere to specific rules and regulations for harmonious living.
  • Members may benefit from lower housing costs due to shared resources and collective management.
