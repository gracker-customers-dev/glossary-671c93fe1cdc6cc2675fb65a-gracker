Mortgage

A legal agreement where a borrower secures a loan against real estate property.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, a mortgage is a financial agreement in which a borrower pledges a property as collateral to secure a loan from a lender, typically a bank or financial institution. The borrower receives funds to purchase or improve the property, and in return, agrees to repay the loan amount along with interest over a specified period. If the borrower fails to repay the loan, the lender has the right to take possession of the property through a legal process called foreclosure. Mortgages are commonly used for residential and commercial property purchases and can take various forms, including fixed-rate, floating-rate, or adjustable-rate mortgages. The legal framework governing mortgages in India is primarily outlined in the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, along with the Indian Registration Act, 1908. The process involves several steps, including property valuation, documentation, and registration. Understanding the terms and conditions of a mortgage is crucial for borrowers, as it affects their financial obligations and rights regarding the property.

Examples

  • A couple takes a home loan to buy their first house and mortgages the property to the bank.
  • An entrepreneur mortgages a commercial property to secure funds for expanding their business.

Additional Information

  • Mortgages can vary in terms of interest rates, repayment schedules, and loan tenures.
  • It is essential for borrowers to check their credit score and loan eligibility before applying for a mortgage.
