IOD

Interest on Deposits

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, IOD stands for 'Interest on Deposits'. It refers to the interest that is earned on the deposits made by buyers or investors in real estate transactions. When a buyer books a property, they often need to make an initial deposit as part of the booking process. This deposit is usually kept in an escrow account or with the developer until the transaction is completed or the property is handed over. During this period, the amount may earn interest, which is typically paid out to the buyer either at the time of possession or as per the agreement terms. The IOD can be an important aspect for buyers as it can add to their overall return on investment. Furthermore, understanding the terms related to IOD can help buyers make informed decisions regarding their investments in real estate, including how deposits are handled and what interest rates apply.

Examples

  • A buyer pays a deposit of ₹10 lakhs for a flat and earns IOD at a rate of 6% until possession.
  • In a commercial property purchase, the buyer deposits ₹50 lakhs and receives interest on the deposit while awaiting completion.

Additional Information

  • IOD rates may vary based on the developer's policies and the current market conditions.
  • Buyers should review the sale agreement to understand the specifics of IOD and its applicability.
