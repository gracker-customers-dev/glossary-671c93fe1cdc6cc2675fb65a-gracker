Corporation

A legal entity created to conduct business, which can own property, enter contracts, and be sued.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a corporation refers to a legal entity established under the Companies Act, 2013, or other applicable laws, that can own, manage, and transact in real estate. Corporations can be involved in various activities such as developing residential or commercial properties, leasing, and property management. They are distinct from partnerships and sole proprietorships, offering limited liability protection to their shareholders, which means that the personal assets of shareholders are typically protected from business liabilities. Corporations can raise capital more easily than other forms of business entities by issuing shares. In real estate development, corporations play a crucial role as they often have the resources and expertise to undertake large-scale projects, and they must comply with regulations set by local municipal corporations and other government bodies. This governance ensures that corporations adhere to zoning laws, environmental regulations, and other legal requirements when dealing with real estate.

Examples

  • DLF Limited is a prominent corporation in the Indian real estate sector known for its large-scale residential and commercial projects.
  • Oberoi Realty Limited is another corporation engaged in the development of premium residential and commercial properties in Mumbai.

Additional Information

  • Corporations must comply with various regulatory requirements, including obtaining licenses and permits for real estate development.
  • The real estate sector in India is significantly influenced by corporate investments, affecting market trends and property values.
