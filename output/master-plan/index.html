Master plan

A comprehensive plan that outlines the long-term vision for development in a specific area.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, a master plan is a strategic framework that outlines the development goals and land use regulations for a specific region or city. It is created by urban planners and local authorities to ensure organized growth, sustainable development, and efficient land use. The master plan typically includes zoning laws, infrastructure development plans, environmental considerations, and public amenities. It serves as a guide for future development projects, helping to coordinate efforts between government agencies, developers, and the community. The master plan is crucial for managing urban sprawl, preserving green spaces, and ensuring that residential, commercial, and industrial developments are balanced. Additionally, it addresses transportation, utilities, and public services, providing a holistic approach to urban planning. In India, master plans are often revised every few years to adapt to changing demographics, economic conditions, and environmental factors.

Examples

  • The Delhi Master Plan 2021 outlines land use and infrastructure development for the capital region.
  • The Mumbai Development Plan 2034 aims to address housing, transportation, and public spaces in Mumbai.

Additional Information

  • Master plans are legally binding and must be adhered to by developers and local authorities.
  • Public participation is often encouraged in the master planning process to ensure community needs are met.
