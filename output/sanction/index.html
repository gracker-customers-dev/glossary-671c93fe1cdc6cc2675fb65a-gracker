Sanction

Approval granted by authorities for a real estate project or construction.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, 'sanction' refers to the formal approval obtained from relevant government authorities before commencing any construction or development project. This process is critical as it ensures that the proposed project complies with local zoning laws, building codes, and environmental regulations. The sanctioning authority varies depending on the type and location of the project and may include municipal corporations, development authorities, and other regulatory bodies. The sanction process typically involves submitting a detailed project plan, including architectural designs, land use plans, and environmental impact assessments. Without obtaining the necessary sanctions, developers may face legal challenges, fines, or demolition of unauthorized structures. Thus, securing the appropriate sanctions is essential for the legality and success of any real estate venture in India.

Examples

  • A builder must obtain a sanction from the local municipal authority before starting construction of a residential complex.
  • An individual seeking to construct a house needs to get a building sanction from the relevant development authority.

Additional Information

  • Sanction includes various approvals such as land use, building plans, and environmental clearances.
  • Failure to obtain proper sanctions can lead to project delays, penalties, or even cancellation.
