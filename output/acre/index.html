Acre

An acre is a unit of area commonly used in the real estate market to measure land.

Description

An acre is a unit of area that is equivalent to 43,560 square feet or approximately 4,047 square meters. In the Indian real estate context, an acre is often used to quantify large parcels of agricultural or residential land. The measurement is significant in land transactions, development planning, and farming activities. One acre is roughly the size of a standard football field, making it a relatable unit for individuals unfamiliar with land measurement. The concept of an acre has historical roots, originally defining the amount of land that could be plowed by a yoke of oxen in one day. In urban areas, land is often sold in smaller units such as square feet, but in rural and semi-urban areas, acres remain a popular measurement. Understanding acres is crucial for buyers and investors in real estate, as it helps in assessing land value, potential for development, and agricultural productivity.

Examples

  • A plot of land for residential development may be advertised as 2 acres.
  • A farmer may own 10 acres of agricultural land.

Additional Information

  • In some regions, land size can also be expressed in hectares, where 1 acre is approximately 0.4047 hectares.
  • Land prices in India can vary significantly based on location, with urban areas commanding higher prices per acre compared to rural areas.
