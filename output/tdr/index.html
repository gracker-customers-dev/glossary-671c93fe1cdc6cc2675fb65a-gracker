TDR

Transfer of Development Rights

Description

Transfer of Development Rights (TDR) is a mechanism used in real estate that allows property owners to transfer the rights to develop their property to another party. This is particularly relevant in urban areas where land is scarce and development needs to be managed more efficiently. TDR is often used to preserve open spaces, heritage sites, or environmentally sensitive areas by compensating property owners through the allocation of development rights that can be sold or transferred. The receiving party can utilize these rights to increase the density or scale of development on their own property. For instance, in a scenario where a government wants to conserve a green space, it might offer TDR to landowners in the vicinity, allowing them to build more on their own lands in exchange for not developing the targeted green area. This system encourages sustainable urban development while ensuring that landowners receive fair compensation for their development rights.

Examples

  • A developer buys TDR from a landowner whose land is designated as a green zone, allowing the developer to build higher-density residential units in another area.
  • A builder obtains TDR from a historical building owner, enabling them to exceed the usual height restrictions on their new construction project.

Additional Information

  • TDR schemes are governed by regulations set by local urban planning authorities.
  • The value of TDR can fluctuate based on demand for development in specific areas.
