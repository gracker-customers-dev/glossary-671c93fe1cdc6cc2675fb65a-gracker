Deposit

A sum of money paid upfront to secure a property in real estate transactions.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, a deposit is a critical component of property transactions. It refers to a sum of money that a buyer pays to the seller or the real estate agent to demonstrate their serious intent to purchase a property. This upfront payment is often a percentage of the total sale price and serves as a legal commitment to the purchase. The deposit is typically paid at the time of signing the sale agreement or when the buyer's offer is accepted. It is an important measure to protect the interests of both parties, as it helps to ensure that the seller takes the property off the market while the buyer arranges for financing or completes due diligence. If the deal goes through, this deposit is usually adjusted against the final purchase price. However, if the buyer backs out without a valid reason, the seller may retain the deposit as compensation for the time and effort invested in the transaction.

Examples

  • A buyer pays a deposit of 10% of the property's value upon signing the sale agreement.
  • In a rental agreement, a tenant may pay a security deposit equivalent to one month's rent.

Additional Information

  • Deposits are often non-refundable unless specified otherwise in the agreement.
  • The amount and terms regarding the deposit can vary based on negotiations between the buyer and seller.
