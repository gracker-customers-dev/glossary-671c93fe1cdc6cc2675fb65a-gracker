Deed

A legal document that conveys ownership of property.

Description

In the context of real estate in India, a 'Deed' is a formal legal document that serves to transfer ownership of a property from one party to another. It is a crucial instrument in property transactions, ensuring that the rights of the seller and buyer are documented and protected. The most common type of deed is the Sale Deed, which outlines the terms of the sale, including the sale price, the parties involved, and the description of the property being sold. Other types of deeds include Gift Deeds, Conveyance Deeds, and Mortgage Deeds, each serving specific purposes in the transfer or encumbrance of property. A deed must be executed on a non-judicial stamp paper of a value determined by the Stamp Act of the respective state in India and must be registered with the local sub-registrar to be legally effective. The registration of the deed provides public notice of the ownership transfer and protects the rights of the new owner against claims from third parties. Therefore, understanding the nuances of deeds is essential for anyone engaging in real estate transactions in India.

Examples

  • Sale Deed: Transfers ownership from seller to buyer.
  • Gift Deed: Transfers property as a gift without consideration.

Additional Information

  • A deed must be signed by both parties and two witnesses to be valid.
  • Failure to register a deed can lead to legal disputes over property ownership.
