PMAY

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana, a government initiative aimed at providing affordable housing.

Description

The Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is a flagship housing scheme launched by the Government of India in June 2015. Its primary objective is to provide affordable housing to the urban poor and economically weaker sections of society. Under this scheme, the government aims to construct 20 million affordable houses by 2022. PMAY focuses on providing housing finance subsidies for the purchase or construction of homes, making it easier for low-income families to own a house. The scheme is divided into two parts: PMAY Urban (PMAY-U) and PMAY Gramin (PMAY-G), addressing the needs of urban and rural populations, respectively. Eligible beneficiaries can avail themselves of interest subsidies on home loans, which significantly reduce the financial burden of purchasing a home. The initiative also promotes the use of sustainable and eco-friendly construction techniques. Through PMAY, the Indian government is striving to achieve the vision of 'Housing for All' by 2022, aiming to enhance the living standard of citizens and foster inclusive growth in the housing sector.

Examples

  • A family earning less than Rs. 3 lakh per annum can receive a subsidy of 6.5% on home loans under PMAY.
  • The scheme supports the construction of affordable housing projects in urban areas, leading to the development of new housing complexes.

Additional Information

  • PMAY is part of the larger 'Housing for All' mission by the Indian government.
  • Beneficiaries can apply online through the official PMAY website for housing assistance.
