Legal description

A legal description is a formal description of a property that is used in legal documents.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, a legal description serves as a precise way to identify a parcel of land. It is a detailed description that provides information about the property's boundaries, location, and dimensions that is recognized in legal contexts. This description can be found in property deeds, sale agreements, and other legal documents. Legal descriptions may use various methods, such as metes and bounds, which describe the property's perimeter using physical boundaries, or lot and block systems, which reference specific lots within a larger subdivision. The legal description is crucial for resolving disputes regarding property ownership, facilitating transactions, and ensuring that the rights of property owners are protected. It must be accurate and must conform to the land records maintained by local authorities. This level of detail ensures that there is no ambiguity about the property being described, which is vital for legal clarity and protection in real estate dealings.

Examples

  • A legal description may state: 'All that piece of land situated at XYZ locality, measuring 60ft by 100ft, bounded by ABC Road to the north.'
  • Another example might include: 'Plot No. 12 in Block A of the ABC Colony, as per the survey map dated DD/MM/YYYY.'

Additional Information

  • Legal descriptions are essential for property registration and conveyancing.
  • In India, it is advisable to consult a legal expert to ensure the correctness of the legal description.
