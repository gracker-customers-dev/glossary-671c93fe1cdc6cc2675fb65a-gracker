Conveyance

The legal process of transferring property ownership from one party to another.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, conveyance refers to the legal procedure through which ownership of property is transferred from one party to another. This process is essential in real estate transactions, as it formalizes the transfer of title and ensures that the new owner has legal rights to the property. The conveyance process typically involves several key steps, including the preparation of a sale deed, registration of the deed with the appropriate government authority, and the payment of stamp duty and registration fees. Proper conveyance is crucial to establish clear title and avoid future disputes regarding property ownership. In India, the conveyance process is governed by various laws, including the Transfer of Property Act and local registration acts. It is advisable for parties involved in a real estate transaction to seek legal assistance to ensure that the conveyance is conducted smoothly and that all requisite documentation is properly handled.

Examples

  • The sale of a residential property where the seller transfers ownership to the buyer through a registered sale deed.
  • Conveyance of commercial property involving the transfer of leasehold rights from one business entity to another.

Additional Information

  • Conveyance can involve various forms of property, including residential, commercial, and agricultural.
  • Failure to properly complete the conveyance process can lead to legal disputes and complications in property ownership.
