Advance

An advance in real estate refers to an initial payment made by a buyer to secure a property before the full payment or transaction is completed.

Description

In the Indian real estate market, an advance is a significant amount paid by the buyer at the time of booking or reserving a property. This payment serves as a token of intent to purchase and is typically a small percentage of the total price of the property. The advance is crucial as it helps the seller take the property off the market, ensuring that the buyer has a claim to it while the remaining transaction process is completed. The advance may be non-refundable if the buyer decides not to proceed with the purchase, which incentivizes serious intent. The terms surrounding the advance, including the amount and its refundability, should be clearly outlined in the sale agreement to avoid disputes. It is common for builders and developers to require an advance payment to initiate the sale process, covering aspects such as paperwork and initial legal formalities.

Examples

  • A buyer pays an advance of 10% of the property value to book an apartment in a new residential project.
  • A tenant pays a month's rent as an advance to secure a rental property before moving in.

Additional Information

  • Advances are usually adjusted against the total property price at the time of final payment.
  • In some cases, specific advance amounts can be negotiated based on market demand and property type.
