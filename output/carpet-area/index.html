Carpet area

The area within the walls of a property that can be covered by a carpet.

Description

In the context of real estate in India, 'Carpet area' refers to the actual usable area within the walls of a residential unit. This measurement excludes the thickness of the walls, balconies, and any common areas shared with other units. It represents the area that can be utilized for placing furniture and other items, providing a clear understanding of the space available to the owner or tenant. Carpet area is a crucial term in the real estate sector as it helps buyers to gauge the actual living space they will have, distinct from other terms like built-up area (which includes walls and other structures) or super built-up area (which includes common areas). The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016, mandates developers to disclose carpet area, which not only promotes transparency but also empowers buyers to make informed decisions. Understanding carpet area is essential for potential homeowners and investors, as it directly impacts the functional use of the property and its overall value.

Examples

  • A 1000 sq ft apartment may have a carpet area of 700 sq ft.
  • In a 3 BHK flat, the carpet area might be around 1200 sq ft, excluding walls and balconies.

Additional Information

  • Carpet area is often used to calculate the price per square foot of a property.
  • Buyers should always verify the carpet area before making a purchase to ensure they are getting value for money.
