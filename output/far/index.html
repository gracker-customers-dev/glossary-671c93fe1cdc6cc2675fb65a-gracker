FAR

Floor Area Ratio - a measurement of a building's total floor area in relation to the size of the land on which it is built.

Description

Floor Area Ratio (FAR) is a critical metric in real estate and urban planning in India, representing the ratio of a building's total floor area to the size of the plot of land it occupies. It is calculated by dividing the total built-up area of all floors of a building by the area of the plot. A higher FAR indicates more construction is allowed on a given piece of land, which can lead to denser development. FAR regulations are set by local governing bodies and vary from one area to another, often influenced by factors such as zoning laws, land use policies, and urban planning objectives. For instance, in residential areas, the FAR is typically lower to maintain a certain aesthetic and living environment, while in commercial zones, a higher FAR may be permitted to support economic growth. Understanding FAR is essential for developers, investors, and buyers as it directly impacts the potential return on investment and the size of the properties that can be constructed.

Examples

  • A plot of 1,000 square meters with a FAR of 2 can have a total built-up area of 2,000 square meters.
  • In a commercial zone, a FAR of 3 might allow for taller buildings compared to a residential zone with a FAR of 1.

Additional Information

  • FAR regulations can be influenced by factors such as environmental impact, infrastructure availability, and community needs.
  • Developers may seek to negotiate higher FAR through special permissions or development incentives in certain projects.
