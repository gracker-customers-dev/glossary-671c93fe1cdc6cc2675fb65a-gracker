Black money

Unaccounted money that is not declared for tax purposes.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, 'black money' refers to income or assets that are generated through illegal means or are not reported to the government for tax purposes. This can include money obtained from corruption, bribery, or illegal activities that is then used to purchase properties without proper documentation or disclosure. The prevalence of black money in real estate often leads to inflated property prices, as buyers may offer higher amounts that include unaccounted cash to secure a deal. This practice not only disrupts the market dynamics but also poses challenges for regulatory authorities trying to monitor and control real estate transactions. Properties bought with black money can complicate ownership rights and create legal issues for future transactions. The Indian government has undertaken various measures to curb the use of black money in real estate, such as implementing the Goods and Services Tax (GST) and the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act (RERA), intending to promote transparency and accountability in property dealings.

Examples

  • A buyer pays a portion of the property price in cash to avoid tax liabilities.
  • A seller inflates the sale price on paper but receives a lower amount in cash.

Additional Information

  • Black money transactions often lack proper documentation and are difficult to trace.
  • Regulatory reforms aim to reduce the role of black money in real estate markets.
