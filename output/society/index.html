Society

A Society is a legal entity formed to manage a group of residential properties or apartments in India.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a 'Society' refers to a cooperative housing society, which is an association of individuals who come together to own and manage a residential property. These societies are typically formed under the Cooperative Societies Act, and they provide a structured framework for residents to govern their living environment. Each member owns a share in the society, which entitles them to occupy a specific unit or apartment within the property. The society is responsible for maintaining common areas, managing finances, and ensuring compliance with local regulations. Members contribute to the society's maintenance fund through monthly or annual fees, which are used for services like security, cleaning, landscaping, and repairs. Societies also facilitate community living by organizing social events and meetings. This structure not only fosters a sense of community but also provides legal protection and support for residents in matters related to property management and ownership.

Examples

  • A housing society in Mumbai that manages multiple apartment blocks.
  • A cooperative society in Delhi that oversees maintenance and community events.

Additional Information

  • Members of a society have voting rights in decision-making processes.
  • Societies must adhere to the rules set forth in their bylaws and relevant state laws.
