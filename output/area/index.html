Area

The total surface measure of a property or land, usually expressed in square feet or square meters.

Description

In the context of real estate in the Indian market, 'area' refers to the measurement of a property, which can significantly affect its value and utility. It encompasses various types of measurements, including built-up area, carpet area, and super built-up area. The carpet area is the actual usable area within the walls of a property, while the built-up area includes the carpet area along with the thickness of the walls and any balconies. The super built-up area incorporates the built-up area plus a proportionate share of common areas, such as hallways and amenities. Understanding the area of a property is crucial for buyers and investors as it helps in assessing the price per square foot and the overall space available for living or business activities. Additionally, area measurements are critical for compliance with local regulations and for determining property taxes. In India, the norms around these measurements can vary by state and developer, making it essential for buyers to clarify these terms before making a purchase.

Examples

  • A 2 BHK apartment with a carpet area of 800 sq. ft.
  • A commercial property with a super built-up area of 1500 sq. ft.

Additional Information

  • Area measurements can impact property valuation and rental prices.
  • Regulations regarding area definitions may differ across different Indian states.
