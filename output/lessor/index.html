Lessor

A lessor is an individual or entity that leases or rents out property to a lessee.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a lessor is the owner of a property who grants the right to use that property to another party, known as the lessee, in exchange for rent. The lease agreement outlines the terms and conditions of the rental arrangement, including the duration of the lease, the amount of rent, and any responsibilities of both parties. The lessor retains ownership of the property and has the right to enforce the terms of the lease, including evicting the lessee if they fail to comply with the lease conditions. In India, the lessor can be an individual, a company, or a government entity. The lessor's rights and obligations are governed by relevant laws, such as the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, and various local regulations. Understanding the role of the lessor is crucial for both landlords and tenants, as it defines the legal framework within which rental transactions occur, ensuring that both parties are aware of their rights and responsibilities.

Examples

  • A homeowner renting out their apartment to a tenant is a lessor.
  • A commercial property owner leasing office space to a business is also considered a lessor.

Additional Information

  • Lessor must provide a legally binding lease agreement to protect their rights.
  • The lessor is responsible for maintaining the property in a habitable condition, unless specified otherwise in the lease.
