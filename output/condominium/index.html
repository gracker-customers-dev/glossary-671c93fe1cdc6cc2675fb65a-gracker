Condominium

A condominium, or condo, is a type of real estate property where individual units are owned separately, while common areas are jointly owned.

Description

In the context of the Indian real estate market, a condominium represents a popular form of housing that combines individual ownership with shared community living. Each unit in a condominium complex is owned by an individual, who holds the title to their specific apartment or unit, while the land and common areas, such as gardens, hallways, and recreational facilities, are owned collectively by all the unit owners through a legal entity typically referred to as the Condominium Association. This arrangement allows residents to enjoy the benefits of home ownership along with shared amenities like swimming pools, gyms, and security services. Condominiums are particularly appealing in urban areas where space is limited, and they offer a more affordable housing option compared to independent homes. Additionally, the maintenance of common areas is typically managed by the Condominium Association, easing the burden on individual owners. The rise of urbanization and increasing housing demands have led to a surge in condominium developments across major cities in India, catering to both domestic buyers and investors.

Examples

  • Prestige Lakeside Habitat in Bangalore
  • Oberoi Sky City in Mumbai

Additional Information

  • Condominiums often have monthly maintenance fees for upkeep of common areas.
  • Ownership in a condominium usually comes with specific rules and regulations set by the Condominium Association.
